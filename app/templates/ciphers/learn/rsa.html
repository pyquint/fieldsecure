What is RSA?

RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is a popular public-key cryptographic technique for safe data encryption and deciphering. It relies on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers, which makes it highly secure. RSA utilizes two keys:

  • A public key for encryption.
  • A private key for decryption.

Thus, ensures that no matter how an attacker intercepts the encrypted message, they are unable to decrypt it without the private key.

Key Generation

Step 1:

Compute $n = p \times q$. $n$ is used as the modulus for both the public and private keys.

$$n = p \times q$$

Step 2:

Compute the totient function, $\phi(n) = (p - 1) \times (q - 1)$.

$$\phi(n) = (p - 1) \times (q - 1)$$

Step 3:

Public Key $e$ : Choose an integer $e$ such that $1 < e < \phi(n)$ and $e$ is coprime with $\phi(n)$.

Common choice are:
$17$ - small, fast for encryption
$65537$ - larger but still efficient (most common today)

Step 4:

Compute $d$ as the modular multiplicative inverse of $e \mod{\phi(n)}$:
$d \equiv e^{-1} \mod{\phi(n)}$ meaning $(e \times d)\mod{\phi(n)} = 1$

if $e = 17$: $$d=2753$$ since $$ 17 \times 2753 \mod{3120} = 1 $$

Step 5:

Display keys.

Public Key: $(e, n)$

Private Key: $(d, n)$
